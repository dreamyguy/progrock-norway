Why?

In mid-2017 I took a deep dive into Norwegian Progressive Rock. I gathered a lot of information about artists from Norway, not only by doing the usual "googling" but also by being an active user in Norwegian forums dedicated to Progressive Rock. It was there I got the most relevant news about upcoming bands and their new releases.

My research resulted in a Spotify playlist, which is embedded below. It is a collection of well established and emerging Progressive Rock bands from Norway, dating from '70s to today. Bear in mind that some bands did not make it to the playlist as they are not on Spotify.

This playlist includes several Progressive Rock sub-genres: Ecletic, Symphonic, Crossover, Space, Hard, Jazz Rock, Folk, Fusion, Experimental, Neo, etc - or just plain good Rock.

Progressive Rock is a thriving genre in Norway. I was very surprised by the number of active bands and the quality of their material. Many artists have new releases, which are usually well received by even the most demanding prog-rockers.

Order of appearance

The artist list is sorted by the order of appearance in the Spotify playlist, but shifts to alphabetical order once the artists are not included in the playlist.

I'll be working on a toggle to sort the whole list alphabetically.

The Criteria (for sorting within playlist)

The sorting in the playlist is set as follows:

Evenness: No artist gets a 2nd track listed before every other artist has been listed.

Fairness: If an artist has a 2nd track, their 3rd isn't listed until every other 2nd track is listed - and so on.

Synergy: I extracted the number of monthly listeners for each artist. The heavy-weights were evenly distributed to support those with less listeners. Emerging and unknown artists were paired with established ones, to the best of my ability.

Omissions (from the playlist)

Even though I was extremely open-minded while creating this list / playlist, some of the artists I came accross were omitted. I wanted to keep the playlist consistent, so when I felt that the band was too pop/metal oriented, I double-checked on their likely publicum through their related artists. I then omitted those artists that had little to no reference to other Progressive Rock bands already in the list.

That does not necessarily mean that the artist you wish to see in this list will never make it to it. I'm still discovering bands, old and new, so I might not have heard of them yet.

Bear in mind that I update the page faster than the playlist itself. This can give the impression that I'm excluding certain bands from the playlist, but that might not be the case. I just need more time to update the playlist.
